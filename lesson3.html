Lesson 3: How RingCT Works

Ring Confidential Transactions (RingCT) are a core privacy feature used in Veil to hide the amount, sender, and recipient of a transaction. Veil combines RingCT with stealth addresses to provide full transactional privacy.

RingCT works by combining your transaction with decoy inputs so that observers can't tell which input is real. It also uses cryptographic commitments to hide the amount being transferred. This makes it difficult to determine who sent what or how much was sent.

Veil integrated RingCT from the privacy project Particl. This allows transparent basecoin transactions to be converted into fully private ones using RingCT, greatly strengthening on-chain privacy. It ensures that users can benefit from advanced privacy technology.

1. What does RingCT hide in a transaction?

Just the sender's address
Only the transaction amount
The sender, receiver, and amount

2. How does RingCT protect sender privacy?

Encrypts the wallet password
Uses decoy inputs to obscure the real input
Delays the transaction broadcast
