Lesson 8: Denominations and Staking Efficiency

Veil's Zerocoin staking system uses fixed denominations when you mint coins. These denominations are 10, 100, 1000, and 10000. You must choose one of these sizes when minting RingCT coins into Zerocoin.

While all denominations are stakeable, larger ones are far more efficient and have a higher chance of producing rewards. This is because:

  • The staking algorithm favors higher-value mints
  • 10000-denomination mints are less fragmented and more likely to be selected
  • Minting lots of small coins can slow staking and increase wallet load

You can see live staking distribution data here.

When possible, you should mint your coins into the highest denomination available. For example, if you have 7000 Veil, you should mint seven 1000 Zerocoin mints. Once you go over 10000 Veil, you can spend the previously minted Zerocoins to yourself, consolidate the balance, and then re-mint them as a single 10000. This increases your chance of staking, reduces fragmentation, and keeps your wallet cleaner.

1. Which Zerocoin denomination is most efficient for staking?

10
100
10000

2. Why is staking with many small denominations not ideal?

It gives higher rewards
It uses more memory and is less efficient
It makes RingCT fail

3. What should a user do before minting for best staking results?

Convert balance into as many 10s as possible
Stake from Basecoin
Consolidate balance and mint a 10000 if possible
